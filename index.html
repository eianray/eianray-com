About

The Short Version

I'm a Certified GIS Professional based in the Pacific Northwest. I've spent nearly two decades helping organizations — from tribal governments to engineering firms to county agencies — build, maintain, and get value out of their geospatial data.

My work spans the practical and the extraordinary. On a typical day, I'm implementing GIS systems for water and sewer districts. On an atypical one, I'm publishing an atlas of Mars or navigating the Arctic in a Porsche Cayenne.

I believe the best GIS work is invisible — infrastructure that just works. The systems I build are meant to outlast the projects that created them.

Things I've Built

GIS Product

DrawBridge

Convert scanned construction drawings (as-builts, plats, utility plans) directly into GIS-ready data. Upload a PDF, digitize features, export as File Geodatabase, Shapefile, GeoJSON, or KML. Built for engineers and GIS professionals who need to get paper into the system.

drawbridgegis.com
API / Developer Tool

Node API

Machine-native GIS processing for AI agents and automated pipelines. 25 geoprocessing operations — convert, reproject, validate, buffer, dissolve, clip, tile — accessible via REST or MCP. Pay-per-operation, no API key required.

nodeapi.ai

Recognition

Publications & Awards

  • The Mars Atlas
    RedMapper · October 2019
    A comprehensive atlas of Martian places and topography, co-developed with an interactive Mars web map. Over 2,000 named features including mountains, craters, valleys, and plains rendered in elevation relief with topographic lines.
  • The Moon Atlas
    RedMapper
    A cartographic atlas of Earth's Moon, featuring over 9,000 named places, 47 human landing artifacts, and full-bleed imagery rendered in the RedMapper cartographic tradition. Map F-10 was featured in Jack Dangermond's 2021 Esri UC Plenary Session.
  • The Mercury Atlas
    RedMapper
    An atlas of Mercury's surface using high-resolution geological overlay and MESSENGER satellite imagery, charting named places and the only known human artifact on the planet.
  • Mapping Mars's Eastern Valles Marineris
    Copernicus Publications / International Cartographic Association · July 2019
    Cartographic representation of an Exploration Zone at the Eastern Valles Marineris, demonstrating that existing data and traditional cartographic methods can produce meaningful geographic discoveries on extraterrestrial surfaces.
